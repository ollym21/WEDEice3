Welcome To Our Page

Mission Statement: "Empowering Unemployed Medical Professionals to Improve Healthcare in Underserved Communities by bridging the gap to a Healthier South Africa"

Our Story

DWJSA was established with the understanding that many trained medical professionals in South Africa are unable to find employment, despite their qualifications. At the same time, many communities—especially in rural areas, townships, and informal settlements—continue to face dire healthcare challenges. We knew that by creating a platform to connect unemployed professionals with healthcare opportunities, we could help address both of these issues, benefiting both individuals and communities alike.

Where We Work

DWJSA targets the most underserved regions in South Africa, including: Rural Areas: Remote areas with little or no access to healthcare facilities. Townships and Informal Settlements: Communities with high poverty rates and limited healthcare infrastructure. Areas with High Rates of Infectious Diseases: Regions most affected by HIV/AIDS, tuberculosis, and other infectious diseases.

Our partnerships

At DWJSA, collaboration is key to achieving our mission. We work closely with: The South African Department of Health: Collaborating on projects and initiatives to align with national healthcare priorities. Private Healthcare Facilities: Providing job placement and training opportunities within private sector hospitals and clinics. Community-Based Organizations and NGOs: Partnering with grassroots organizations to deliver healthcare services directly to underserved communities.

Overview

DWJSA is a non-profit organization that aims to address the issue of unemployment among medical professionals in South Africa, while also improving access to quality healthcare in underserved communities.
